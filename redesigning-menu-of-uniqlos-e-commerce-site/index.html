Redesigning menu of Uniqlo’s e-commerce site

Project | Uniqlo
Role | UX research & UI design
Duration | Nov 2021 (2-3 weeks)
Problem

The e-commerce site needs a better user experience, particularly in navigating

Uniqlo has been one of the popular fashion brands among Malaysians due to its simple yet good-looking, and high-quality apparels. It offers in-store and online shopping that ease everyone to choose their preferred way to shop.

As more products and features launched, the e-commerce site is getting more difficult to maintain. To fix the most significant user problem, I conducted research to have a better understanding.

In fact, the interface of Uniqlo’s e-commerce site is different based on countries. In this project, I aimed to improve the site targeting Malaysians due to the familiarity with my country of birth.


Side note: I do not work for Uniqlo, and the views from this case study are strictly my own.

Research

Heuristic evaluation

I visited the site through desktop, tablet and mobile in order to evaluate any usability issues that affect online shopping experience. I jotted down the findings in MindNote to have a clear overview.

In short, the core problem of Uniqlo’s site is the poorly organised information architecture. The site still works, but is difficult to navigate. It is also lack of inspiration.

Issue prioritisation

I used this simple graph to prioritise on issues which fell into essential and low effort categories.

Competitive analysis

Based on Uniqlo’s Industry Ranking in 2021, I studied the e-commerce sites of five of the major competitors, all of which are global apparel manufacturer and retailer brands.

The analysis was compiled in a spreadsheet to organise and compare the data. Strengths and weaknesses of the menu were recorded. Below were the most common findings among competitors.

Strengths
  • Clearly organised second-level menu items
  • Simple and clean design
  • Use mega menu on desktop with relevant images
  • Use sequential menu with smooth transition on mobile
Weakness
  • Some design unoptimised on different devices

Before jumping into digital design, I quickly sketched a few wireframes to revamp a more organised and inspiring menu layout for different devices. The main focus for the new design was on desktop and mobile versions only, it would then be adapted for tablet from mobile.

Test design with usability study

Both existing and new Uniqlo users were invited to study the usability of the current menu on Uniqlo’s site and the prototyped new menu.

I tested desktop menu before proceeding to mobile menu to study if its usability on one device was optimised. This approach also prevented the same mistake happened in mobile menu’s usability study since the design changes after testing on desktop menu would also be applied to mobile menu before testing.

Limitation

Although the study allowed testing if an menu item was findable, it was not able to test if an menu item was discoverable. This means that it did not reflect the most true experience of a user navigating a site’s menu.

Definition (by Nielsen Norman Group):

  • Findability: Users can easily find content or functionality that they assume is present in a website.
  • Discoverability: Users encounter new content or functionality that they were not aware of previously.

Result

Navigating on the menu after redesigning showed a more positive result

When comparing the current Uniqlo’s site menu and the new menu, users were able to find the items easier and faster on the latter.

On desktop

The success rate to find an menu item was also higher for new design since all items were above the fold and discoverable. Added labels also aided users to better understand the complex menu structure.

On mobile

The usability study with existing Uniqlo users showed that the experience on both design was similar while with new users showed that the experience on new design was better in providing clearer navigation. This distinction might be due to their familiarity with the Uniqlo menu structure.

Next Steps

The next issues to fix are those fell into essential and high effort categories.

Takeaways

I thought that redesigning the menu structure was simple, but I underestimated it. It actually took quiet some time in studying the existing structure, fashion industry terms, and trends among competitors in order to categorise things in a way the majority can understand.
