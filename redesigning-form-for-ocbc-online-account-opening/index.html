Redesigning form for OCBC Online Account Opening

Project | OCBC
Role | UI design
Duration | Aug - Oct 2020 (2 months)
Problem

The client needs a more user-friendly way for online banking account application

This project aimed to combine and ease the online application process of two main saving accounts of OCBC bank, which are 360 and Boost accounts. Besides that, my team was asked to redesign the whole process in order to look more welcoming.

We went through several discussions with our client to understand their most important needs, which were:


  • To break down the existing form’s long structure into smaller steps
  • A new design with a modern and minimalistic look
Research

Heuristic evaluation

The existing form to apply for 360 Account had all the input fields in one page, causing it to be extremely long. Considering that much effort in filling in the form, applicants were prone to abondon it at the first place. It also frustrated them whenever errors occur as they could not target the errors easily.

The form before redesign (desktop)
Design & Result

User flow

After breaking down the existing structure, the user flow was revamped to introduce the new 5 steps focusing on from the most general to most sensitive information.

Final mocks with responsive design

The new form design had preserved OCBC’s corporate identity and infused a fresh and spacious look for better desktop and mobile interaction.

With the new steps, a progress bar was also added to inform users about their status of completion. This also helped users to better understand the context and focus on what they need to fill in for OCBC Online Account Opening.

The form after redesign (desktop)
The form after redesign (mobile)

Takeaways

During the redesign progress, the earlier version of my design had a more distrinct structure than the existing form which would delay the development timeline. This projects taught me that getting developers involved in the early stage can avoid wasting time on unnecessary work.

Better if ...

It could be better if usability study was conducted before launching the new design to seek for further improvement.

To study the actual impact of the new design, the ideal way is to compare the completion rate and error rate of account application before and after the redesign through analytics software such as Google Analytics.
